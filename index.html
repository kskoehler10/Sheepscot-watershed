About this project ►

The flow-distance raster was derived from a 10 m resolution LiDAR-based DEM using GRASS GIS r.watershed. Each cell's value is the cumulative least-cost flow distance that a raindrop falling on that cell has to travel downhill to reach the mouth of the Sheepscot River at the Route 1 bridge in Wiscasset, ME.

The stream network was extracted with r.stream.extract and ordered using the Strahler system: headwater channels are order 1; the Sheepscot River mainstem reaches order 8. Line widths scale with order, reflecting the accumulation of upstream drainage area.

Waterbodies are from the NHD (National Hydrography Dataset, HUC 01050003). Roads, trails, and named waterways are from OpenStreetMap.
